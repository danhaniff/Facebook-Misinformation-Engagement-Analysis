# Facebook News Engagement & Misinformation Analysis (Tableau Project)

## Overview
This project analyzes a Facebook political news dataset to uncover patterns in user engagement and content distribution. Using Tableau, the analysis focuses on how different types of content and levels of factual accuracy influence user behavior, including shares, reactions, and comments.

The goal is to demonstrate how data visualization and descriptive analytics can reveal insights about misinformation and engagement dynamics on social media platforms.

---

## Dataset
- Source: Facebook political news dataset (course-provided)
- Size: ~2,200+ records
- Content: Posts categorized by type (link, video, photo, text) and truthfulness rating

---

## Tools & Technologies
- Tableau (data visualization & dashboards)
- Descriptive Analytics (mean, median, standard deviation)
- Data Cleaning (handling missing values and outliers)

---

## Key Analysis Areas

### 1. Engagement Metrics Distribution
- Analyzed share counts, reaction counts, and comment counts
- Found heavily right-skewed distributions across all metrics
- Viral posts significantly increase averages and create extreme outliers

### 2. Post Type Distribution
- Link posts dominate the dataset (~78% of all content)
- Video and photo posts make up a smaller portion but drive significantly higher engagement

### 3. Falsehood Rating Analysis
- Majority of posts classified as “mostly true” (~73%)
- ~15% of posts contain some level of false or misleading information
- “No factual content” posts generate the highest engagement levels

---

## Key Findings
- Engagement on social media is driven by a small number of viral posts
- Visual content (photos/videos) significantly outperforms link-based content
- Opinion-based and non-factual content generates higher engagement than factual reporting
- Data distributions are highly skewed, making median more reliable than mean for interpretation

---

## Business & Cybersecurity Implications
This analysis highlights important challenges in misinformation and digital communication:

- Emotionally driven content spreads faster than factual information
- Social media platforms may amplify misleading or opinion-based content
- Organizations must consider engagement bias when analyzing online data
- Understanding these patterns is critical for combating misinformation and improving digital trust
